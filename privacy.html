Privacy Policy

Your data stays yours.

Sora is a local app. It doesn't have servers, accounts, or analytics. There's nothing to collect and nowhere to send it.

🚫

No data collected

Sora never collects, stores, or transmits any personal information.

📡

No servers

There are no Sora servers. Everything stays on your Mac, always.

🔓

Open source

The full source code is on GitHub. Verify every claim yourself.

Data Collection

What we collect

Nothing. Sora does not collect any data about you, your browsing, or your device. There are no analytics, no crash reporters, no telemetry, and no usage tracking of any kind.

Local Storage

What stays on your Mac

Sora stores the following data locally on your device, in your user directory. None of it ever leaves your Mac.

  • Browsing history — stored locally, clearable at any time from Settings → History
  • Saved passwords — stored in your macOS Keychain, never in plaintext
  • Site permissions — per-domain camera, mic, and JavaScript preferences saved to a local JSON file
  • Settings & preferences — theme, search engine, and other preferences stored via UserDefaults
  • Favicons — cached locally to avoid re-fetching on every visit

Network Requests

What Sora connects to

Sora makes network requests on your behalf when you browse. The only first-party network request Sora makes automatically is fetching favicons from Google's favicon service when you visit a new site. Everything else is a direct result of your navigation.

  • Search suggestions — sent to your chosen search engine as you type in the address bar
  • Favicons — fetched from favicons.googleapis.com for visual tab identification
  • Update checks — handled by Sparkle, Apple's standard macOS update framework, which checks GitHub releases

Third Parties

Third-party websites

When you visit a website in Sora, that website's own privacy policy applies. Sora's built-in ad and tracker blocking reduces the number of third parties that can observe your activity, but we have no control over the sites you visit.

Updates

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be committed to the public GitHub repository with a clear description of what changed. Last updated: March 2026.

Questions about privacy? The source code is the most honest answer — but you can also open an issue on GitHub.
